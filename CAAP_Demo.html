Clinically Aware Alert Prioritization

Why CVSS alone fails
in smart hospitals

CVSS measures technical severity. It cannot tell the difference between an attack on an ICU ventilator and an attack on a cafeteria PC. CAAP adds five clinical dimensions that CVSS ignores entirely.

The CAS Formula — Clinical Alert Score (0–10)

CAS  =  (0.25 × TR + 0.30 × CC + 0.25 × TS + 0.10 × AE + 0.10 × TC) × 2

Each dimension is scored 0–5 · multiplied by 2 to produce a 0–10 final score matching CVSS convention

TR
Threat Risk
0.25
Random Forest predict_proba confidence mapped to 1–5. High confidence attack prediction → high TR.
CC
Clinical Criticality
0.30
Device cc_score column (1–10) halved → 1–5. ICU Ventilator = 5. Admin PC = 0.5. Highest weight in formula.
TS
Time Sensitivity
0.25
Isolation Forest anomaly score + time_sensitivity column. Anomalous traffic → elevated TS floor.
AE
Active Exploitation
0.10
CVE lookup per attack class. DoS_TCP = 5, ARP Spoofing = 4, Recon = 2, Benign = 0.
TC
Temporal Context
0.10
shift column from dataset. Night = 5 (minimal staff), Evening = 3, Day = 1. Attack at 3am is more dangerous.

CVSS v3.1 — what it measures

  • Attack vector (network / adjacent / local)
  • Attack complexity
  • Privileges required
  • User interaction required
  • Confidentiality / Integrity / Availability impact
  • Zero awareness of device clinical role
  • Zero awareness of patient dependency
  • Zero awareness of hospital shift or staff levels

CAAP CAS — what it adds

  • All CVSS technical data via TR dimension
  • Device type (ventilator vs admin PC)
  • Clinical criticality from cc_score column
  • Patient dependency (life_critical / high / low / none)
  • Anomaly behaviour from Isolation Forest
  • Attack class severity per known IoMT CVEs
  • Time of day / hospital shift (staff availability)
  • Result: alerts sorted by patient safety impact, not abstract technical score
